Make Small Rooms Look Bigger

1. Use Mirrors

Mirrors reflect light and create the illusion of more space. Place a large mirror opposite a window to maximize natural light and visually expand the room.

2. Choose Light Colors

Opt for soft tones like white, beige, or pale gray. These colors reflect light better than dark shades, making your room feel airy and open.

3. Use Multi-functional Furniture

Invest in furniture that serves more than one purpose—like a bed with storage drawers or a fold-out table. This reduces clutter and maximizes usable space.

4. Arrange Furniture Wisely

Keep walkways clear and avoid overfilling the room. Floating furniture a few inches away from the wall can also create a sense of space.

5. Emphasize Vertical Space

Use tall shelves, curtains hung close to the ceiling, or vertical stripes on walls to draw the eye upward, making ceilings appear higher.
